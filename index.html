About Queso the Bearded Dragon

🦎 Who is Queso?

Queso is our beloved bearded dragon who serves as an ambassador for reptile education. Named after his golden-yellow coloration that resembles melted cheese, Queso helps us teach others about proper bearded dragon care and the fascinating world of these incredible reptiles.

📚 Educational Mission

Through Queso's story, we aim to educate potential and current bearded dragon owners about proper care, habitat requirements, nutrition, and the joy these amazing creatures can bring to our lives when properly cared for.

🏠 Queso's Home

Queso lives in a carefully designed 75-gallon terrarium with proper UVB lighting, temperature gradients, and enrichment items. His habitat demonstrates the ideal setup for bearded dragon wellness and happiness.

Amazing Bearded Dragon Facts

  • Scientific Name: Pogona vitticeps - they're native to Australia's arid regions
  • Temperature Regulation: Bearded dragons are ectothermic and need external heat sources to regulate their body temperature
  • Third Eye: They have a photosensitive organ called a parietal eye on top of their head that detects shadows and light changes
  • Communication: They communicate through arm waving, head bobbing, and beard darkening
  • Diet: Omnivores that eat both insects and vegetation, with diet changing as they age
  • Lifespan: Can live 10-15 years in captivity with proper care
  • Brumation: Like hibernation, they may enter a dormant state during cooler months
  • Swimming: Despite living in arid regions, they're surprisingly good swimmers!

Bearded Dragon Care Guide

🏠 Habitat

75+ gallon tank, proper substrate, hiding spots, basking area, and climbing branches

🌡️ Temperature

Basking spot: 95-105°F, Cool side: 75-85°F, Night: 65-75°F

💡 Lighting

UVB lighting 10-12 hours daily, replace bulbs every 6-12 months

🥗 Diet

Juveniles: 80% insects, 20% vegetables. Adults: 20% insects, 80% vegetables

💧 Hydration

Fresh water dish, regular baths, misting vegetables for extra hydration

🏥 Health

Regular vet checkups, watch for signs of illness, maintain proper hygiene
