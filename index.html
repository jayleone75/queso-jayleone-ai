Essential Care Guide

Feeding

  • Feed juveniles 2-3 times daily
  • Adults eat once daily or every other day
  • 80% vegetables, 20% insects for adults
  • Dust insects with calcium powder
  • Provide fresh water daily
  • Remove uneaten food promptly

Temperature

  • Basking spot: 100-110°F
  • Cool side: 80-90°F
  • Night temperature: 70-75°F
  • Use ceramic heat emitters at night
  • Digital thermometers for accuracy
  • Temperature gradient is crucial

Lighting

  • 12 hours of UVB lighting daily
  • Replace UVB bulbs every 6-12 months
  • UVB helps calcium absorption
  • Natural sunlight is beneficial
  • Avoid glass barriers with UVB
  • Use timer for consistent schedule

Humidity

  • Maintain 30-40% humidity
  • Use digital hygrometer
  • Provide occasional baths
  • Mist enclosure lightly if needed
  • Good ventilation prevents mold
  • Monitor during shedding periods

Enclosure

  • 40-gallon minimum for babies
  • 75+ gallons for adults
  • 4x2x2 feet ideal adult size
  • Secure screen top
  • Multiple hiding spots
  • Easy to clean surfaces

Health

  • Annual vet checkups
  • Watch for lethargy or appetite loss
  • Monitor bowel movements
  • Check for stuck shed
  • Quarantine new additions
  • Keep detailed health records

Perfect Habitat Setup

Heating

Basking bulbs, ceramic heat emitters, and under-tank heaters to create proper temperature gradients throughout the enclosure.

UVB Lighting

Full-spectrum UVB lights are essential for calcium absorption and preventing metabolic bone disease.

Substrate

Reptile carpet, paper towels, or tile work best. Avoid sand and loose substrates that can cause impaction.

Decorations

Branches for basking and climbing, caves for hiding, and safe plants for enrichment and natural feel.

Monitoring

Digital thermometers, hygrometers, and timers to maintain consistent environmental conditions.

Water & Food

Shallow water dishes and ceramic food bowls that are easy to clean and won't tip over easily.

Amazing Bearded Dragon Facts

Color Communication

Bearded dragons can change their color to regulate temperature and communicate emotions. Darker colors absorb more heat, while lighter colors reflect it.

Third Eye

They have a primitive third eye (parietal eye) on top of their head that can detect shadows and changes in light from above, helping them spot aerial predators.

Arm Waving

The arm waving behavior is a sign of submission or acknowledgment, often seen when they recognize their owner or encounter another bearded dragon.

Swimming Ability

Despite being desert dwellers, bearded dragons are surprisingly good swimmers and can hold their breath underwater for several minutes.

Brumation

Like hibernation in mammals, bearded dragons undergo brumation - a period of dormancy during cooler months where they sleep for weeks or months.

Glass Surfing

When they run along glass walls, it's called "glass surfing" and can indicate stress, boredom, or seeing their reflection.
